# Business Insights & Findings

What the data actually says, and what a business should do about it.

Based on analysis of 1,800 orders across Jan–Dec 2024.

---

## Revenue Overview

Total revenue for the year came in at ₹298.68L with a profit margin of 49.7%. That margin is well above the typical Indian e-commerce retail average of 15–25%, which reflects the mix of high-margin Fashion and Furniture products alongside lower-margin Grocery items.

Average order value was ₹16,593, pulled up significantly by Electronics orders (Laptops at ₹45,000, Smartphones at ₹22,000).

---

## Finding 1 — Fashion has the best margin, not Electronics

Electronics drives 38.6% of total revenue but only achieves a 44.1% effective margin after discounts. Fashion contributes a smaller revenue share but runs at 63.8% margin — the highest of all five categories. Grocery sits at the bottom at 32.4%.

**What this means:** The business should not discount Electronics heavily, because margins are already the thinnest of the high-revenue categories. Fashion, on the other hand, can absorb promotional discounts and still deliver strong profit.

**Action:** Run discount campaigns only on Fashion and Books, where margins allow it. Protect Electronics pricing.

---

## Finding 2 — Laptop alone accounts for a disproportionate share of revenue

Laptop is the top product by revenue at ₹72.3L, more than 1.5× the next product (Sofa at ₹45.2L). A business this reliant on a single SKU has concentration risk — a stock issue or price change for one product meaningfully affects the whole company's numbers.

**Action:** Grow the Smartphone and Monitor revenue by running bundles (Laptop + Keyboard + Headphones) to increase cross-sell while reducing single-product dependency.

---

## Finding 3 — 43 Champion customers, and 7 are already at risk

RFM segmentation identified:
- **43 Champions** — bought recently, buy often, spend the most
- **70 Loyal** — consistent buyers but not top spenders
- **7 At-Risk** — previously active customers who have not purchased recently

The 7 At-Risk customers are the most urgent. They have demonstrated willingness to spend (they were Champions or Loyal before) but have gone quiet. Win-back is significantly cheaper than new customer acquisition.

**Action:** Send a targeted win-back offer to the 7 At-Risk customers specifically — a 10% discount on a category they have purchased before. For Champions, no discount is needed — they buy anyway. A loyalty recognition email ("You're one of our top customers") is enough.

---

## Finding 4 — Hyderabad and Bangalore lead revenue, but smaller cities are under-indexed

Hyderabad leads at ₹41.2L, Bangalore at ₹39.9L. Both are tech-heavy metros with high disposable income. Cities like Jaipur and Lucknow trail significantly despite having real purchasing power.

**What this likely means:** Lower-tier cities are not being marketed to effectively, not that demand is absent.

**Action:** Test a targeted Electronics campaign in Jaipur and Lucknow with free delivery as the hook. If conversion rates are comparable to metro cities, expand budget allocation there.

---

## Finding 5 — No seasonal spike in Q4

Monthly revenue is remarkably flat — ranging from ₹22L to ₹26.7L with no significant Diwali or year-end spike. For an Indian e-commerce business, this is unusual. Flipkart and Amazon India both see 3–5× revenue spikes in October–November.

**What this means:** The business has not run a proper festive season campaign, or the dataset period predates any such effort.

**Action:** Plan a dedicated Diwali campaign (October 1 – November 15) with gift bundles across Electronics and Fashion. Pre-stock Laptop, Smartphone, and Saree 6 weeks in advance. A 25–30% Q4 revenue lift over the current flat baseline is a realistic target.

---

## Finding 6 — Discounts are being applied too broadly

15% discounts appear on high-margin Fashion items and on expensive Electronics orders where a 5% discount already reduces profit by ₹2,250 per Laptop unit.

**Action:** Cap Electronics discounts at 5%. Reserve 10–15% discounts for At-Risk customers in win-back campaigns. Do not apply discounts to Champions who show no price sensitivity in their purchase history.

---

## Priority Actions Summary

| Priority | Action | Expected Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| High | Win-back campaign for 7 At-Risk customers | Recover 5–7 customers before they churn permanently |
| High | Cap Electronics discounts at 5% | Immediate margin improvement on highest-revenue category |
| Medium | Diwali bundle campaign (Oct–Nov) | 25–30% Q4 revenue lift over flat baseline |
| Medium | Expand into Jaipur and Lucknow via Electronics ads | New revenue from under-served markets |
| Low | Add 4–6 new Fashion SKUs | Increase share of highest-margin category |
